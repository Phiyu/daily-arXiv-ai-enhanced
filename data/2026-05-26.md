TL;DR: 该研究利用背景恒星的光学偏振观测，分析九个亮缘云（BRCs）和彗状球状体（CGs）外围弥散包层中尘埃颗粒的偏振效率与排列机制，发现偏振效率随消光增加而降低，并在磁场有序区域随尘埃温度略有上升，支持RAT（辐射力矩）对齐机制。

Motivation: 星际磁场对分子云结构演化至关重要，而通过非球形尘埃颗粒在磁场中对齐产生的星光和热尘埃偏振是探测磁场形态与强度的关键手段。然而，尘埃颗粒的具体对齐机制尚不明确，主流理论为RAT理论，因此需通过观测验证其适用性。

Method: 作者利用Gaia EDR3和StarHorse 2星表提供的恒星距离与消光数据，结合投射到九个BRCs/CGs方向上的背景恒星的光学偏振观测，分析偏振度、偏振位置角及消光随恒星距离的变化，识别偏振尘埃层，并计算各云的偏振效率及其与消光、尘埃温度和磁场有序度的关系。

Result: 部分云在特定距离处出现消光突增并伴随偏振度上升，表明存在偏振尘埃层；偏振效率普遍随消光增加而下降；在磁场更有序的云中，偏振效率随尘埃温度略有上升，支持RAT对齐机制；而在磁场方向扰动较大的云中，偏振效率下降可能源于磁场无序。

Conclusion: 观测结果总体上支持RAT理论作为尘埃颗粒对齐机制，同时揭示了磁场结构对偏振效率的重要影响，强调在解释偏振观测时需综合考虑尘埃物理性质与磁场几何形态。

Abstract: The polarization of starlight and thermal dust emission, resulting from non-spherical grains aligned with the interstellar magnetic field (B-field), act as a powerful tool to trace the B-field morphologies and strengths in molecular clouds and constrain the grain alignment mechanisms and grain properties. The exact alignment mechanisms of grains is not yet fully clear. However, the leading theory is the alignment induced by RAdiative Torques (RATs), known as RAT theory. In this work, we use optical polarization observations of background stars projected towards nine of Bright-Rimmed Clouds (BRCs) and Cometary Globules(CGs) to study the polarization efficiencies and the alignment mechanisms of the grains in the direction towards the outer diffuse envelopes of these clouds. We use distance and extinction data of the stars from Gaia EDR3 and StarHorse 2 Catalogue. We study the variations of the degree and position angle of polarization, and the extinction, as functions of distance of the stars. For some of the clouds, we find discrete enhancement of the extinction at certain distances along with an increase in polarization degree, signifying the presence of polarizing dust layers. We estimate the polarization efficiency of grains towards each of the clouds. We find that it decreases with increasing extinction, and also shows a slight increase with dust temperature for some clouds associated with more ordered magnetic field orientations, providing an implication for the alignment of grains by RATs. Whereas, for some other clouds, the decrease in the polarization efficiency with extinction may be caused by more fluctuations in the magnetic field orientations.
